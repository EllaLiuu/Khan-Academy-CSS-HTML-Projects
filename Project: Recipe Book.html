Crème Brûlée

About

Use this amazing Crème Brûlée recipe to make a delicious looking and tasting Crème Brûlée. Not only is this recipe extremly easy to follow, but it's also quite easy to make! Crème Brûlée has always been something that seems daunting or hard to make but it's actually quite simple! Give this recipe a try to see how effortless it really is!

  • Prep time: 30 minutes
  • Cook time: 40 minutes
  • Chilling time: 4 hours
  • Yield: 6 servings

Ingredients

Ingredient Quantity
Heavy cream 2 cups
Sugar 14 tablespoons
Vanilla bean 1
Egg yolk 4
Vanilla extract 1/2 teaspoon
Kosher salt 1/4 teaspoon

Special Equipement

  • 9x13 baking pan
  • 6 4-ounce ramekins
  • Fine mesh strainer
  • Whisk
  • Two mixing bowls
  • Crème brûlée torch

Directions

Step 1: Preheat oven to 325°F with a rack in the middle position. Arrange six 4-ounce ramekins in a 9x13 baking dish and set aside.
Step 2:Prepare the vanilla bean: Split the vanilla bean in half and scrape out the seeds with the dull (non-cutting) side of a paring knife.
Step 3: Warm the cream: In a medium saucepan, combine heavy cream, 4 tablespoons of the sugar, vanilla bean pod, vanilla bean seeds, and salt. Cook over medium heat, until it begins to simmer.
Step 4: Infuse the cream: Once the cream begins to simmer, remove the pan from heat, cover, and let stand for 15 minutes to allow the vanilla bean to infuse into the cream. (If you’re replacing the vanilla bean with vanilla extract, skip this step.)
Step 5: Separate the eggs and whisk with sugar: When your cream is almost done infusing, separate your eggs. Place the yolks in a medium-sized mixing bowl; save the whites for another purpose.
Step 6: Temper the yolks and mix with the cream: While whisking the yolks continuously, slowly add one ladle (about 1/4 or 1/3 cup) of hot cream in a thin stream. Do this two or three more times. While whisking, slowly add the remaining cream to the egg mixture, followed by the vanilla extract. Strain the mixture through a fine mesh sieve into a second mixing bowl and discard the vanilla bean pod (this also helps strain out any small bits of egg that may have curdled). At this point, the custard can be cooled and stored for up to 5 days. If you choose to bake them right from the fridge, you will probably need to add a couple of extra minutes.
Step 7: Fill the ramekins: Divide the custard between the ramekins, filling each one just up to the inner rim, or about three-quarters of the way full.
Step 8: Fill the hot water bath: Bring a kettle or pot of water to a boil. Fill the pan with the hot water until the water comes about two-thirds of the way up the side of the ramekins. Avoid splashing water into the custards.
Step 9: Bake the custards: Transfer the pan with the custards to the oven and bake for 38 to 40 minutes. When ready, the crème brûlée should have a uniform jiggle in the middle; when you touch the center, it should spring back just a bit. If the sides hold firm but the center sloshes, they aren’t quite done yet. If they are set like Jell-O, they’ve over-baked, although they will still be delicious! Chill them, brûlée them, eat them, love them and celebrate your efforts. If they have overbaked to the point that they look risen in the center, toss them, start again, and celebrate your efforts!
Step 10: Cool and chill the custards: Remove the baking dish with the finished custards from the oven and set on a wire cooling rack. Let the custards cool in the water until you can safely pick up the ramekins by the rim with your fingers. This should take about 15 minutes, depending on how tender your fingers are. (Mine are like asbestos, so I tend to go in rather quickly.) Once removed from the water bath, let each custard rest on the cooling rack for about 15 more minutes, then cover each ramekin with plastic wrap and store in the fridge. Chill for at least 4 hours, or up to 4 days, before serving.
Step 11: Brûlée the custards: When ready to serve, remove the custards from fridge. Sprinkle the surface of each custard evenly with about 1 teaspoon of granulated sugar. Rotate the custard as you sprinkle to make sure the sugar evenly coats the surface. Ignite your torch. Hold the flame about 4 inches from the surface of the sugar. Using a circular motion, move the flame across the surface. The sugar will at first begin to bead up, and then will eventually solidify into a hard sugar crust. You want to make sure all the sugar granules have melted, and the sugar has at least a tint of caramel color. The longer you torch the surface, the darker it will become and the more bitter the sugar crust will be. Each of the custards with an additional teaspoon of sugar and brûlée the surface again.
Step 12: Serve the crème brûlée: Cool for at 2 minutes, and serve within 20 minutes. After about 20 minutes, the sugar will begin to soften, but if it takes you longer to get them to the table, don’t fret. You'll get less of a hard "crack!" when breaking up the surface of the crème brûlée, but it will still be good. To eat, use the edge of your spoon to gently crack the hard sugar surface and scoop up some custard. Be sure to get both the brittle topping and the creamy custard in each bite.

Source: Simply Recipes

Lasagna

About

Lasagna is a such a rich and delicious dish. Although it seems hard to make, it's actually very simple! This recipe allows you to make lasagna quickly and very easily. This is a highly rated recipe with 4.5 stars so I highly suggest that you try out this dish, trust me you won't regret it!

  • Prep time: 35 minutes
  • Cook time: 1 hour
  • Yield: 8 servings
  • Calories: 702

Ingredients

Ingredient Quantity
Lean ground beef 1 pound
Part-skim ricotta cheese 1 pint
Onion, chopped 1
Grated Parmesan cheese 1/4 cup
Mushroom, drained 1 can
Eggs 2
Spaghetti sauce 1 jar
Lasagna noodle 1 package
Cottage cheese 1 package
Shredded mozzarella cheese 8 ounces

Special Equipement

  • Baking dish
  • Large skillet
  • Medium sized bowl
  • Aluminum foil

Directions

Step 1: Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
Step 2: In a large skillet, cook and stir ground beef until brown. Add mushrooms and onions; saute until onions are transparent. Stir in pasta sauce, and heat through.
Step 3: In a medium size bowl, combine cottage cheese, ricotta cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, and eggs.
Step 4:Spread a thin layer of the meat sauce in the bottom of a 13x9 inch pan. Layer with uncooked lasagna noodles, cheese mixture, mozzarella cheese, and meat sauce. Continue layering until all ingredients are used, reserving 1/2 cup mozzarella. Cover pan with aluminum foil.
Step 5: Bake in preheated oven for 45 minutes. Uncover, and top with remaining half cup of mozzarella cheese. Bake for an additional 15 minutes. Remove from oven, and let stand 10 to 15 minutes before serving.

Pancakes

About

Although pancakes are very delicious they are actually very easy to make. This basic pancake recipe will give you easy intsructions so that you can whip up a delicious breakfast or brunch for your friends, family or even just for yourself!

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 5 minutes
  • Yield: 4 servings

Ingredients

Ingredient Quantity
Flour 1 cup
Sugar 2 tablespoons
Baking powder 1 tablespoon
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Egg 1
Milk 1 cup
Oil 2 tablespoons

Special Equipement

  • Ladle
  • Large skillet
  • Medium sized bowl

Directions

Step 1: Mix first 4 ingredients.
Step 2: Beat egg, milk and oil in medium bowl with whisk until blended. Stir in flour mixture.
Step 3: Ladle batter onto hot griddle or into hot skillet sprayed with cooking spray, using 1/4 cup batter for each pancake. Cook on medium heat until bubbles begin to form on tops, then turn to brown other sides.

Source: Kraft Canada

Macaroni & Cheese

About

Macaroni and cheese is a common favourite and staple dish. Most people tend to eat it out a box but that's not always the healthiest and can be a bit costy. However, with this recipe you can easily make macaroni and cheese from scractch, simply and quickly! Once you make this, you will never go back to frozen or boxed macaroni and cheese!

  • Prep time: 5 minutes
  • Cook time: 25 minutes
  • Yield: 6 servings

Ingredients

Ingredient Quantity
Elbow macaroni 16 ounces
Butter 1/4 cup
Flour 1/4 cup
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Black pepper 1 dash
Milk 2 cup
Shredded cheese 2 cups

Special Equipement

  • Medium sauce pan
  • Wooden spoon

Directions

Step 1: Cook macaroni according to package directions.
Step 2: In medium saucepan, melt butter over medium heat; stir in flour, and cook for 3-5 minutes to form a roux; add salt and pepper; slowly add milk, stirring well after each addition.
Step 3: Cook and stir until bubbly.
Step 4: Stir in cheese a small amount at a time until fully melted.
Step 5: Drain macaroni; add to cheese sauce; stir to coat.

Source: Genuis Kitchen
